Focus Areas

My study concentrated on questions at the intersection of technology and ethics. I am observing an AI boom unfold while examining philosophical frameworks for understanding power, knowledge, and social structures. Conversations with colleagues explored connections between computational systems, ethics, and theology.

My independent research comes from the lens of a first-generation Mexican-American engineer: understanding both how technical systems function and how they impact communities underrepresented in technology development.

Guiding Questions

  • "Who builds AI systems versus who experiences their consequences?"
  • "What does technological literacy look like in a modern world and how do we reshape critical thinking skills?"
  • "How do we talk about AI ethics in communities that lack the technical background to understand what these systems are?"
  • "What responsibility do engineers have to the communities their work affects?"

Current Engagement

Recent conversations with faculty and professionals have explored the intersection of large language models and weapons technology, technological literacy gaps in underrepresented communities, and the unequal distribution of AI's benefits and harms. These discussions have confirmed the value of perspectives that bridge technical capability with lived experience outside of mainstream technology development.

My approach combines formal computer science training with self-directed philosophical study, offering a perspective on technology's impact that comes from understanding both system architecture and social consequence.
